Title: ISU Dining Meal Plans and Rates
Source Type: Official ISU Dining page
Source URL: https://www.dining.iastate.edu/meal-plans/
Date Collected: June 9, 2026

Content:
The ISU Dining meal plan page explains official meal plan options for Iowa State students. Meal plans are built around dining center meals, GET & Go or Express meals, Dining Dollars, guest meals, and sometimes Flex Meals depending on the plan.

The Cardinal Plan is described as giving students unlimited access to dining centers for breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks. Official search results for the Cardinal Plan list unlimited dining center access, GET & Go two meals per day, $250 Dining Dollars per semester, five guest dining center meals per semester, and a listed semester price for 2025-2026 and 2026-2027.

The Iowa State catalog describes the Gold Plan as a declining meal balance plan. It says Gold gives students 210 dining center meals each semester along with Dining Dollars. The catalog also says each plan gives students a set number of Dining Dollars and GET & Go meals.

Dining Dollars spend dollar-for-dollar at ISU Dining locations and are useful for snacks, drinks, cafes, markets, convenience items, and retail dining. ISU Dining FAQ results say Cardinal, Gold, and Campanile plans include Dining Dollars, and students can add more Dining Dollars if they run short. Dining Dollars expire at the end of the spring semester.

GET & Go lets students use one dining center swipe for a quick meal at one of five GET & Go locations. The Iowa State catalog says students order through the GET app and can use dining center meals at GET & Go locations up to two times a day.

This source is useful for factual meal plan questions, such as what Cardinal includes, how Gold works, what Dining Dollars are, what GET & Go does, and how meal plan benefits differ. Student Reddit threads are needed to answer whether students think the plans are actually worth it.
